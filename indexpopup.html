Location

The Cang mountain range is located at the southern end of the eastern branch of the Yunling Mountains, for which Yunnan is named. These mountains are part of the greater Hengduan Mountains(横断山脉) which stretch from the Yunnan-Guizhou Plateau to the Tibetan Plateau. Cangshan is approximately 50 km in length, running north-south, and 12 km wide.


Cangshan is a fault-block mountain and was formed by the uplifting and sinking of the earth’s plates along this fault line. It contains more than 10 types of metamorphic rocks such as granulite, migmatite, schist, slate, and marble. The marble of Cangshan, known as Dali marble (大理石), is widely known for its beautiful patterns which can mirror those of traditional Chinese landscape paintings.



Cangshan’s past glaciers are unique as being the southernmost point of paleo-glaciation in China. This glaciation, which took place 57.6 and then 16 thousand years ago, dramatically altered Cangshan’s landscape at an altitude above 3500 meters. Large glacial remnants, such as cirques, horns, arêtes, and glacial lakes can all be seen as one approaches the mountain’s peaks. Click on the green mountain icons on the map to learn more about some of these features.
